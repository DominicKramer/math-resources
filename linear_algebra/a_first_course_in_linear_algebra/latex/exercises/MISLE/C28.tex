Let
%
\begin{equation*}
C=
\begin{bmatrix}
 1 & 1 & 3 & 1\\
 -2 & -1 & -4 & -1\\
 1 & 4 & 10 & 2\\
 -2 & 0 & -4 & 5
\end{bmatrix}
\end{equation*}
%
Compute the inverse of $C$, $\inverse{C}$, by forming the $4\times 8$ matrix $\augmented{C}{I_4}$ and row-reducing (\acronymref{theorem}{CINM}).  Then use a calculator to compute $\inverse{C}$ directly.
