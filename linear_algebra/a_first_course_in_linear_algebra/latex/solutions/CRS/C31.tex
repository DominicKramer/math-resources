\acronymref{theorem}{BCS} is the right tool for this problem.  Row-reduce this matrix, identify the pivot columns and then grab the corresponding columns of $A$ for the set $T$.  The matrix $A$ row-reduces to
%
\begin{equation*}
\begin{bmatrix}
 \leading{1} & 0 & 3 & 0 & 0 \\
 0 & \leading{1} & -2 & 0 & 0 \\
 0 & 0 & 0 & \leading{1} & 0 \\
 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & \leading{1}
\end{bmatrix}
\end{equation*}
%
So $D=\set{1,\,2,\,4,\,5}$ and then
%
\begin{equation*}
T=\set{\vect{A}_1,\,\vect{A}_2,\,\vect{A}_4,\,\vect{A}_5}=
\set{
\colvector{2 \\ 1 \\ -1 \\ 2},\,
\colvector{1 \\ -1 \\ 2 \\ -1},\,
\colvector{-1 \\ 1 \\ 0 \\ -1},\,
\colvector{2 \\ 1 \\ 1 \\ 2}
}
%
\end{equation*}
%
has the requested properties.
