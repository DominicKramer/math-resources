Row reduce $A$,
%
\begin{equation*}
A\rref
\begin{bmatrix}
 \leading{1} & 0 & 0 & 1 & 1 \\
 0 & \leading{1} & 0 & -3 & -1 \\
 0 & 0 & \leading{1} & -2 & -2 \\
 0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0
\end{bmatrix}
\end{equation*}
%
So $r=3$ for this matrix.  Then
%
\begin{align*}
\dimension{\nsp{A}}
&=\nullity{A}&&\text{\acronymref{definition}{NOM}}\\
&=\left(\nullity{A}+\rank{A}\right)-\rank{A}\\
&=5-\rank{A}&&\text{\acronymref{theorem}{RPNC}}\\
&=5-3&&\text{\acronymref{theorem}{CRN}}\\
&=2
\end{align*}
%
We could also use \acronymref{theorem}{BNS} and create a basis for $\nsp{A}$ with $n-r=5-3=2$ vectors (because the solutions are described with 2 free variables) and arrive at the dimension as the size of this basis.
