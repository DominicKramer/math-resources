The matrix row-reduces to
\begin{equation*}
\begin{bmatrix}
\leading{1}&  0 &  0 &  0\\ 
0 & \leading{1} &  0 &  0\\ 
0 &  0 & \leading{1} &  0\\ 
0 &  0 &  0 & \leading{1}
\end{bmatrix}
\end{equation*}
which is the $4\times 4$ identity matrix.  By \acronymref{theorem}{NMRRI} the original matrix must be nonsingular.
