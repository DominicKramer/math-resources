We can expand about any row or column, so the zero entry in the middle of the last row is attractive.  Let's expand about column 2.  By \acronymref{theorem}{DER} and \acronymref{theorem}{DEC} you will get the same result by expanding about a different row or column.  We will use \acronymref{theorem}{DMST} twice.
%
\begin{align*}
\begin{vmatrix}
3 & -1 & 4\\
2 & 5 & 1\\
2 & 0 & 6
\end{vmatrix}
&=
(-1)(-1)^{1+2}
\begin{vmatrix}
2 &  1\\
2 &  6
\end{vmatrix}
+
(5)(-1)^{2+2}
\begin{vmatrix}
3 &  4\\
2 &  6
\end{vmatrix}
+
(0)(-1)^{3+2}
\begin{vmatrix}
3 &  4\\
2 &  1
\end{vmatrix}\\
%
&=(1)(10)+(5)(10)+0=60
%
\end{align*}
